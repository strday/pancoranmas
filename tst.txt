**What is Stunting?**

* Stunting refers to chronic or recurrent malnutrition in children. It results in a child being too short for their age.
* The World Health Organization (WHO) defines stunting as a height-for-age that is more than two standard deviations below the WHO Child Growth Standards median. 

**Consequences of Stunting**

Stunting has far-reaching and severe consequences for both individuals and entire societies:

* **Individual Consequences:**
    * Reduced cognitive development and learning potential
    * Weakened immune systems, leading to increased susceptibility to illness 
    * Increased risk of chronic diseases later in life (e.g., diabetes, heart disease)
    * Lowered economic productivity in adulthood

* **Societal Consequences**
    * Strains on healthcare systems due to increased illness rates
    * Losses in economic potential and productivity
    * Perpetuation of poverty and inequality

**Global Impact of Stunting**

* Stunting is a widespread global problem, affecting millions of children worldwide. It is especially prevalent in low- and middle-income countries.
* Approximately 149 million children under the age of five are stunted (2020).
* Stunting contributes to child mortality and hinders the overall development of nations.
